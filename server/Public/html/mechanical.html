MCE - 206

Computer Aided Design and Manufacturing

This course explores the integration of computer technology in the design and manufacturing process. It covers CAD software for 3D modeling, simulation, and analysis, as well as CAM techniques for automated production. Students learn how to streamline product development, improve precision, and enhance productivity in modern manufacturing environments.

MEC - 200

Kinematics and Structure of Machines

This course delves into the motion and structure of mechanical systems. It examines the principles of kinematics, dynamics, and design of machine components such as gears, cams, and linkages. Students learn to analyze motion, force transmission, and structural integrity to optimize machine performance.

MEC - 303

Thermal Energy Systems

This course focuses on the principles of thermodynamics and heat transfer in energy systems. It covers the design, analysis, and optimization of thermal systems, including power plants, refrigeration, and HVAC systems. Students learn to enhance energy efficiency and sustainability in thermal energy applications.

MEC - 304

Integrated Mechanical Laboratory - I

This hands-on laboratory course provides practical experience in mechanical engineering concepts. Students perform experiments related to dynamics, fluid mechanics, and thermodynamics, enhancing their analytical and problem-solving skills through real-world applications.

MEC - 302

Design,Materials and Manufacturing

This course explores the relationship between design, material selection, and manufacturing processes. It covers product design principles, material properties, and modern manufacturing techniques, enabling students to create efficient, sustainable, and cost-effective products.

MEC - 300

Control Engineering Theory and Applications

Focusing on the fundamentals of control systems, this course covers modeling, analysis, and design of control systems for mechanical and electrical applications. Students learn to develop stable and responsive systems using feedback and control strategies.

MEC - 405

Learning Factory Project

This project-based course simulates real-world manufacturing environments, where students collaborate to design, prototype, and optimize products. It emphasizes teamwork, problem-solving, and practical application of engineering principles in a learning factory setting.

MEC - 100

The World of a Mechanical Engineer

This introductory course provides an overview of the diverse roles and responsibilities of mechanical engineers. It explores career opportunities, industry trends, and the impact of engineering solutions on society, preparing students for their future professional journey.

MEC - 307

Mechanics of solids and designing with materials

This course covers the behavior of solid materials under various forces and conditions. It includes stress analysis, deformation, and failure theories, guiding students to design safe and efficient structures using appropriate materials.

MEC - 308

Mechanics of Machines, Vibrations and Structures

This course examines the motion and forces in mechanical systems, focusing on machine dynamics, vibrations, and structural stability. Students learn to analyze and design mechanisms that minimize vibrations and ensure structural integrity.
